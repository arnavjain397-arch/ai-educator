# CLAUDE.md — ai-educator

## Who I Am
I'm Arnav, and this repo is my personal AI education curriculum. My goal is to become a top 1% Claude and AI practitioner — someone who can build, evaluate, and reason about AI systems at a deep level, not just prompt them.

## What This Repo Is For
Structured, self-directed learning through sessions, exercises, notes, and applied projects. Each folder has a purpose:
- `sessions/` — logs and transcripts from learning sessions
- `exercises/` — hands-on practice problems
- `notes/` — written summaries of topics studied
- `projects/` — larger applied projects demonstrating mastery

## My Coding Preferences
- **Simple over clever** — if there's a straightforward way, use it
- **Well-commented** — explain the why, not just the what
- **Clean and readable** — consistent style, no unnecessary complexity
- **No over-engineering** — don't abstract until there's a clear need

## My Learning Goals
- Deeply understand how Claude and LLMs work, not just how to use them
- Build real projects that demonstrate AI engineering skills
- Develop strong intuitions for prompt design, model behavior, and system architecture
- Reach a level where I can evaluate, critique, and improve AI systems

## Rules for How Claude Should Communicate With Me
- Be concise and direct — skip filler and preamble
- Teach, don't just do — explain what you're doing and why when it's a learning moment
- Flag when I'm doing something suboptimal, even if I didn't ask
- Use plain language first; introduce jargon only when it adds precision
- Don't pad responses — short and clear beats long and thorough
- When writing code, always explain any non-obvious choices
- Treat me as a capable learner who can handle honest feedback
